Connectivity & Fibre Reseller — Sales Process

Field agents, gazebo setups, township community engagement. Partners: Telkom, Supersonic, Home Connect, ND Connect via Openserve infrastructure.

Connect
Show up in the community. Be at the gazebo, hand out flyers, knock on doors. Fibre sells when people see a real human being from the neighbourhood explaining it.
·
Communicate
Speak their language. Compare ISP packages in plain rands per month. Answer "will it work in my area?" before they ask.
·
Converse
Handle objections like a neighbour, not a salesperson. Follow up until they're on fibre — that's the whole goal.

Step-by-Step Detail
01
Lead Generation — Go to the Community
Field Agent
  1. Set up branded gazebo in a high-foot-traffic area inside a confirmed Openserve coverage zone.
  2. Distribute flyers door-to-door in the surrounding 200m radius. Focus on flats, complexes, and informal rows first.
  3. Engage passers-by with a simple opener: "Do you have Wi-Fi at home? Can I show you what fibre costs in your area?"
  4. Log every conversation in the tracking sheet — even the "not interested" ones (area, objection, date).
  5. Ask every existing customer for one referral before leaving their home after installation.
Output: Lead list with name, contact number, address, and initial interest level (Hot / Warm / Cold).
⚠ Bottleneck: Team runs out of flyers mid-day — always pre-pack 200+ per session.
02
Coverage Verification — Check Before You Promise
Field Agent
  1. Open the Openserve coverage checker on your phone before engaging the lead further.
  2. Enter the client's exact street address. Screenshot the result.
  3. If covered: move forward immediately. Say — "Good news, your street is already on fibre."
  4. If not covered: add to a waitlist with the client's number and tell them honestly — "We'll call you the moment fibre reaches your street."
  5. Never promise a date for coverage that doesn't exist yet. One broken promise kills word-of-mouth.
Output: Coverage status confirmed. Waitlist updated if not covered.
🔴 Risk: Promising coverage that doesn't exist. Resolution: Coverage check is non-negotiable — no check, no pitch.
03
ISP Package Comparison — Help Them Choose
Field Agent
  1. Pull up the ISP comparison sheet (always keep the latest printed copy in your bag too).
  2. Ask: "How many people use the internet at home? Do you stream? Work from home?"
  3. Based on their answers, recommend 1–2 packages. Don't overwhelm with all options.
  4. Always mention the month-to-month option first — it reduces the fear of commitment.
  5. If they want Telkom — explain the SIB commission structure means you earn your first full month. Process it properly through the Telkom portal only.
Output: Client has selected an ISP and package. Verbal commitment confirmed.
✅ Efficiency: Keep a laminated one-page ISP comparison card — no scrolling, no fumbling.
04
Application & Documentation — Get It Right First Time
Field Agent / Admin
  1. Collect: SA ID or passport, proof of address (utility bill, lease, bank statement — not older than 3 months).
  2. Complete the ISP application form with the client present. Read it back to them.
  3. Take clear, well-lit photos of all documents — blurry scans are rejected and cause delays.
  4. Get the client's preferred installation time window (morning / afternoon / weekend).
  5. Confirm contact number twice — one digit wrong means the technician can't reach them.
Output: Complete application package ready for submission. All documents verified on the spot.
🔴 Risk: Incomplete docs = rejected application = client frustration. Resolution: Checklist on your phone before you leave the client.
05
Order Submission — Into the System
Admin / Field Agent
  1. Log into the correct ISP partner portal within 2 hours of collecting the application.
  2. Submit all documents and fill in the application form exactly as the client's documents show — no shortcuts.
  3. Note the reference number immediately. Save it in the CRM against the client's record.
  4. Send the client a WhatsApp: "Hi [Name], your application is in. Reference: [XXX]. We'll contact you once your installation date is confirmed."
  5. Follow up on the portal every 48 hours if no confirmation has come through.
Output: Order live in ISP system. Reference number logged. Client notified.
⚠ Bottleneck: Portal downtime. Resolution: Save the ISP contact number — call to submit verbally if the portal is down for more than 4 hours.
06
Installation Coordination — Don't Let It Fall Through
Admin / Field Agent
  1. Once Openserve confirms the installation date, call the client immediately — don't rely on SMS alone.
  2. Remind the client 24 hours before: "Your technician is coming tomorrow between [time]. Please be home."
  3. If the client misses the appointment, there's usually a rescheduling delay of 1–2 weeks. Make this clear upfront — it helps them take it seriously.
  4. If the technician doesn't show, escalate to the ISP within the same day. Do not wait for the client to chase.
Output: Installation confirmed and client ready to receive technician.
🔴 Risk: No-shows (both sides). Resolution: Call the client the morning of, not just the night before.
07
Activation & Handover — Make Sure It Works
Field Agent (if present) / Admin
  1. Call the client on the day of installation to confirm it happened and the line is live.
  2. If you're on-site: run a speed test. Make sure the speed matches the package they signed up for.
  3. Walk the client through connecting their first device. Many first-time fibre users don't know where the Wi-Fi password is.
  4. Send a WhatsApp 24 hours after activation: "Hey [Name], just checking your fibre is running well. Any issues?"
Output: Client is live and satisfied. Service confirmed working. Record updated in CRM as "Active".
✅ Efficiency: Happy clients at activation = referrals. Ask immediately — the feeling is fresh.
08
Referral Ask — Grow From Within
Field Agent
  1. At the moment the client is happiest (right after activation), ask: "Do you have a neighbour or family member who'd also want fibre? I'll sort them out the same way."
  2. Offer a simple referral reward if applicable (data voucher, airtime) — keep it honest and consistent.
  3. Log the referred contact immediately. Don't trust memory.
  4. Treat every referral lead like a hot lead — they already trust you because someone they know does.
Output: At least 1 referral contact per successful activation. Logged as a warm lead.
✅ Mindset: The best sales happen between neighbours. One satisfied customer in a complex can unlock the whole building.
Risks, Bottlenecks & Efficiency

🔴 Key Risks

  • Promising coverage that doesn't existFix: Coverage check is step one. Non-negotiable.
  • Incomplete application docsFix: Physical checklist per client before leaving.
  • Client not home for installationFix: Confirm the morning of, not just the night before.
  • Technician no-show not escalatedFix: Onea escalates to ISP same day. We don't wait.
  • Wrong ISP portal usedFix: One agent = one assigned portal login. Labelled clearly.

⚠️ Bottlenecks

  • Flyers run out mid-sessionFix: Minimum 200 per agent per session. Restock weekly.
  • ISP portal goes downFix: ISP ops contact on speed dial for verbal submissions.
  • Applications sit in someone's bagFix: Submit within 2 hours of collection — hard rule.
  • CRM not updated after stepsFix: Update the record before you move to the next client. Not at end of day.

✅ Efficiency Fixes

  • Laminated ISP comparison cardOne page. Current prices. Always in the bag.
  • WhatsApp template messagesPre-written for: application confirmation, installation reminder, activation check-in.
  • Weekly lead reviewEvery Monday: review warm leads, escalate stuck applications, check waitlist.

💜 Team Experience

  • Pain: No feedback on whether applications were approvedFix: Assign one admin to track portal status and update the agent by WhatsApp.
  • Pain: Clients call the agent about installation, not adminFix: Client receives admin contact at application stage, not the agent's personal number.
  • Pain: Agent unsure which ISP to pushFix: Simple decision tree card — 3 questions → 1 recommendation.

Digital Marketing & Communications Agency — Sales Process

Strategy, creative, media buying, campaigns, PR, social media, content, branding. Clients range from SMEs to corporates. Minimum 70% deposit on all projects.

Connect
The discovery call is not a sales pitch. It's a listening session. Understand their business before you open a slide deck.
·
Communicate
Your proposal must speak their language — business outcomes, not marketing jargon. Tell them what they'll get, not what you'll do.
·
Converse
Objections are part of the process. Stay calm, ask questions, and solve the real concern. Don't discount. Restructure the scope.

Step-by-Step Detail
01
Lead Comes In — Capture It Immediately
BDM / Account Manager
  1. Every lead — WhatsApp, email, referral, DM, website form — goes into the CRM within 1 hour of receipt. No exceptions.
  2. Log the source (where they came from), the name, company, and what they're asking about.
  3. Send an acknowledgement within 2 hours: "Thanks for reaching out. I'll be in touch by [time] to understand what you need."
  4. Assign a priority: Hot (budget + timeline ready), Warm (interested, exploring), Cold (just browsing).
Output: Lead logged in CRM. Acknowledged. Priority assigned.
⚠ Bottleneck: Leads sitting in WhatsApp without being logged. Fix: Dedicated CRM contact method — not a personal number.
02
Qualify the Lead — Know Before You Commit Time
BDM / Account Manager
  1. Ask the four qualifying questions in a casual conversation or a short WhatsApp exchange:
    — What do you need help with?
    — When do you need it done?
    — Do you have a budget in mind?
    — Are you the person who makes the final call?
  2. If the answer to question 4 is no — ask to include the decision-maker in the discovery call.
  3. If they have no budget whatsoever, send them our pricing guide and check back in 2 weeks.
Output: Lead is qualified or routed to nurture. Time is protected.
🔴 Risk: Hours spent on proposals for people who can't say yes. Resolution: Qualify before you build anything.
03
Discovery Call — Listen More Than You Talk
BDM / Account Manager / CEO
  1. Book a 30–45 minute call or meeting. Send a calendar invite with a brief agenda so they're prepared.
  2. Open the call: "Before I tell you anything about us, help me understand your business." Then listen.
  3. Take notes in the discovery brief template — not on random paper.
  4. Ask about: current marketing, what's not working, who their customers are, biggest business challenge right now.
  5. End with: "Based on what you've told me, I think we can help with [X]. I'll send a proposal by [date]."
Output: Completed discovery brief. Clear understanding of what the client needs and why.
✅ Mindset: Every discovery call is a Connect moment. If they feel heard, they'll trust the proposal.
04
Proposal Build — Solve Their Problem, Not Ours
Account Manager + Creative Lead
  1. Use the discovery brief as the foundation. The proposal must reference things the client actually said.
  2. Structure: Executive Summary → Their Problem → Our Solution → Deliverables → Timeline → Pricing → Next Steps.
  3. Pricing from the approved price list only. No improvised quotes. If in doubt, check with the CEO first.
  4. Include a payment terms page clearly: 70% deposit on acceptance, 30% on delivery/sign-off.
  5. Get the proposal reviewed internally before sending — one fresh set of eyes catches errors.
  6. Send as a PDF. Always. Not as a link that can expire.
Output: Professional, branded proposal PDF sent to client within agreed timeframe.
🔴 Risk: Under-pricing from gut feel. Resolution: Use the pricing workbook. Always.
05
Present the Proposal — Walk Through It Together
BDM / Account Manager
  1. Never just email a proposal and hope for the best. Book a call to walk through it.
  2. Open: "I'll take you through this section by section. Stop me any time you have questions."
  3. At the pricing slide, don't apologise for the number. Present it confidently.
  4. Watch for hesitation or silence at the pricing section — that's a signal to ask: "Does the scope feel right, or is there a part you'd like us to adjust?"
  5. Always end with a clear close: "What would you need to see from our side to move forward?"
Output: Proposal presented. Feedback captured. Next step agreed on the call.
⚠ Bottleneck: Client ghosts after receiving the proposal. Fix: Book the presentation call before sending the PDF.
06
Objection Handling — Stay in the Conversation
BDM / Account Manager
  1. The most common objections and how to handle them:
    — "It's too expensive" → Offer to reduce scope, not price. Protect the rate card.
    — "We need to think about it" → Ask what's holding them back. Set a follow-up date on the call.
    — "We're getting other quotes" → Ask what criteria they'll use to decide. Then show how Onea meets those.
    — "We don't have budget right now" → Ask when their next financial period starts. Log for follow-up.
  2. Never give a discount without giving something back (reduced scope, shorter timeline).
Output: Objection addressed. Revised proposal sent if needed. Client moves to next step.
✅ Mindset: Objections are Converse moments. They're not saying no — they're asking you to convince them.
07
Contract & Deposit — Close It Properly
Account Manager / CEO
  1. Send the SLA/contract the same day the client says yes — don't let 24 hours pass.
  2. Contract must include: scope of work, deliverables, timelines, payment terms (70% deposit, 30% on delivery), revision rounds, and IP ownership.
  3. Send the invoice for the deposit immediately with the contract. One email, both attachments.
  4. Confirm work starts when the deposit clears. Not before. This is a firm rule — no exceptions.
  5. Once payment is received, send a receipt and a welcome message to the client.
Output: Signed contract on file. 70% deposit received. Project starts.
🔴 Risk: Starting work before the deposit clears. Resolution: Zero exceptions. The contract makes this clear from proposal stage.
08
Onboarding — Set the Project Up for Success
Account Manager + Delivery Team
  1. Hold an internal briefing with the delivery team within 24 hours of the deposit clearing.
  2. Share the full discovery brief and signed proposal with the team. No verbal briefings alone.
  3. Send the client a kick-off email with: project start date, key milestones, their point of contact at Onea, and what you'll need from them (brand assets, login access, approvals).
  4. Set up a shared folder or project board and invite the client.
  5. Book the first progress check-in 2 weeks out — not when something goes wrong.
Output: Project live. Team briefed. Client informed. Milestones set.
✅ Efficiency: A strong onboarding prevents scope creep. Clients who know what to expect, ask for less.
Risks, Bottlenecks & Efficiency

🔴 Key Risks

  • Starting work without a depositFix: Non-negotiable policy. Invoice and contract go together.
  • Scope creep eroding marginFix: Revision rounds clearly stated in contract. Extra rounds = extra invoice.
  • Decision-maker not in discovery callFix: Qualify who the decision-maker is before booking the call.
  • Quoting from memory, not the price listFix: Every quote goes through the pricing workbook. No exceptions.

⚠️ Bottlenecks

  • Proposal takes too long to buildFix: Use the proposal template. Discovery brief feeds straight into it.
  • Client ghosts after receiving proposalFix: Book the presentation call before you send the PDF.
  • CEO is the only person who can approve proposalsFix: Define a threshold — proposals under a set value can be sent by AM without CEO sign-off.

✅ Efficiency Fixes

  • Proposal templateOne template for all service types. Fill in the blanks from the discovery brief.
  • Pre-written follow-up sequencesDay 1, Day 3, Day 7 after sending a proposal — in HubSpot.
  • Contract template ready to sendFill in scope, pricing, dates. Sign electronically. Same day as verbal yes.

💜 Team Experience

  • Pain: Unclear on what to say when a client pushes back on priceFix: Objection handling cheat sheet — printed and on every desk.
  • Pain: Proposals take forever because team doesn't have the pricingFix: Pricing workbook is always accessible. Shared drive, not someone's laptop.
  • Pain: Not knowing where a deal is in the pipelineFix: HubSpot pipeline stages — visible to the whole team in real time.

IT Services — Sales Process

Network cabling, infrastructure, cloud, ERP, hardware supply, software licensing, IT support. Technical sales that require precision and credibility from the first conversation.

Connect
IT clients buy from people who understand their environment. Get on-site early. Build trust before you build a proposal.
·
Communicate
Technical specs matter, but the decision-maker cares about downtime, cost, and peace of mind. Translate tech into business impact.
·
Converse
Procurement is rarely one call. Be patient. Stay present. A client who asks questions is a client who's buying — help them get there.

Step-by-Step Detail
01
Lead Identified — Know Where It's Coming From
BDM / CTO / CEO
  1. Log the lead immediately — source (tender portal, referral, existing client expansion, cold outreach), contact person, company size, and what they're asking about.
  2. For tenders: confirm the submission deadline immediately. Add it to the calendar with a 5-day early-warning reminder.
  3. For referrals: send a thank-you to the referrer within 24 hours. This keeps the pipeline flowing.
  4. Assign the lead to the right person: CTO for technical scoping, BDM for commercial pursuit.
Output: Lead logged, source captured, responsible person assigned, deadline noted.
🔴 Risk: Tender deadlines missed. Resolution: Tender leads get a calendar entry and a responsible owner the same hour they're received.
02
Technical Discovery — Understand the Environment
CTO / Senior Dev
  1. Request a technical discovery meeting or call — 45 to 60 minutes with the client's IT manager or operations lead.
  2. Find out: current infrastructure, existing systems, pain points, compliance requirements (POPIA, ISO, etc.), number of users/endpoints, and any previous failed solutions.
  3. Document everything in the technical brief template. Don't rely on memory for technical environments.
  4. At the end of discovery, confirm: "Based on what you've shared, we'll do a site visit and come back to you with a detailed quotation."
Output: Technical brief complete. Clear picture of what the client needs and the environment we're working in.
✅ Mindset: A discovery that uncovers pain is a discovery that closes deals. Listen for what's frustrating them right now.
03
Site Assessment — See It With Your Own Eyes
CTO / Senior Technician
  1. Book the site visit with a defined purpose: "We're coming to assess [X] so we can scope the work accurately."
  2. Bring the site assessment checklist. Take photos and measurements where needed.
  3. For cabling projects: count cable runs, identify conduit routes, assess patch panel locations, note power points.
  4. For network/cloud: document current equipment (make, model, age), internet link type, and physical server room conditions.
  5. For hardware supply: capture exact specs required — don't spec from memory.
  6. Never quote on-site. Take the information away and build the quote properly.
Output: Completed site assessment report. Photos on file. Data for accurate quoting.
🔴 Risk: Quoting without a site visit leads to scope underestimation and margin erosion. Fix: Site visit is mandatory for all infrastructure and cabling work.
04
Quotation — Build It Right
CTO / Account Manager
  1. Use the IT pricing workbook. Every line item — hardware, software licensing, labour, transport, consumables — must be captured.
  2. Apply the correct margin: hardware min 15%, labour min 20%, software licensing as per vendor agreement.
  3. Separate your quote into sections: Hardware Supply, Software & Licensing, Labour & Installation, Post-installation Support (optional).
  4. State lead times clearly for hardware — if stock isn't confirmed, say so. Overpromising on ETD is a trust killer.
  5. Include the payment terms: 70% on PO, 30% on delivery/sign-off. Always.
  6. Get CTO sign-off before sending. Technical accuracy is non-negotiable.
Output: Professional, accurate quotation with itemised BOM, labour, and payment terms. CTO approved.
⚠ Bottleneck: Quotations delayed because one person holds all the supplier pricing. Fix: Shared supplier price list, updated monthly, accessible to the CTO and AM.
05
PO & Deposit — Formalise Before You Move
Account Manager / CEO
  1. For corporate clients: request a Purchase Order before or alongside the deposit invoice.
  2. Send the deposit invoice the same day as the signed quote/approval. Don't wait.
  3. Procurement starts when the deposit clears. Communicate this to the client upfront — it manages expectations on lead times.
  4. For tender contracts: follow the client's payment process exactly. Understand their 30/60/90-day payment terms before you commit to a project timeline.
Output: PO received, deposit invoice sent, payment confirmed, procurement authorised.
🔴 Risk: Procuring hardware before payment clears. Fix: No procurement without deposit. Cash flow rule — not just policy.
06
Procurement & Scheduling — Coordinate Everything
CTO / Admin
  1. Place hardware orders with confirmed suppliers immediately after deposit. Log order reference numbers.
  2. Confirm delivery ETD from the supplier within 24 hours of ordering.
  3. Communicate the installation timeline to the client: "Hardware arrives [date], installation scheduled for [date]."
  4. Book the technician(s) for the installation date. Don't assume availability — confirm in writing.
  5. Inspect hardware on receipt before the installation date. Don't arrive on-site with faulty equipment.
Output: Hardware confirmed in hand. Installation date booked. Client and technician aligned.
⚠ Bottleneck: Hardware delays that aren't communicated to the client. Fix: Update the client the day the ETD changes — not when they ask.
07
Delivery & Installation — Execute Cleanly
CTO / Technicians
  1. Arrive on time. Bring all tools, equipment, and consumables. Don't make a second trip for something forgotten.
  2. Brief the client on-site before starting: what you're doing, how long it'll take, who to speak to if there's a question.
  3. Test every deliverable before you leave. Cabling: run tests on all points. Network: test speed and failover. Hardware: boot up and confirm specs.
  4. If something doesn't work on-site and can't be fixed immediately, tell the client honestly. Give a resolution timeline and stick to it.
  5. Get the sign-off form completed before the technician leaves. Signature = delivery confirmed.
Output: Work complete, tested, and signed off by client. Remaining 30% invoice triggered.
🔴 Risk: Leaving without a signed completion form. Fix: Completion sign-off is mandatory before the final invoice is issued.
08
Support & Upsell — Stay in the Account
Account Manager / CTO
  1. Send a check-in message 5 business days after delivery: "Just checking everything is running well. Any issues we can help with?"
  2. Offer a support or maintenance SLA while the relationship is warm. It's easier to retain than to re-acquire.
  3. At the 30-day mark: review the project with the client. What worked, what could improve. Use this for your own process improvement too.
  4. Ask: "Is there anything else in your environment you'd like us to look at?" — This is where Phase 2 projects come from.
  5. Log all upsell opportunities in the CRM as new pipeline items.
Output: Client satisfied, SLA offered, next opportunity identified and logged.
✅ Mindset: The IT client who's happy at sign-off is worth three referrals and at least one follow-on project.
Risks, Bottlenecks & Efficiency

🔴 Key Risks

  • Missing a tender deadlineFix: Calendar entry + 5-day warning the minute a tender is identified.
  • Quoting without a site visitFix: Site visit mandatory for all infra work. No exceptions.
  • Hardware procurement before depositFix: Procurement is only authorised after proof of payment.
  • No signed completion formFix: Final invoice doesn't go out without the completion sign-off. Full stop.

⚠️ Bottlenecks

  • Quotations held up by one person's scheduleFix: CTO has 24 hours to review. If unavailable, CEO signs off on commercial, CTO confirms tech specs separately.
  • Supplier pricing not currentFix: Shared supplier price list. Updated monthly. One person owns the update.
  • Hardware delays not communicatedFix: Any ETD change gets communicated to the client before they ask.

✅ Efficiency Fixes

  • Site assessment checklistRole-specific — one for cabling, one for network, one for hardware delivery. Always in the bag.
  • Technical brief templateFill this in during the discovery call. It feeds directly into the quotation.
  • Completion sign-off formPre-printed. On every job. Final invoice doesn't move without it.

💜 Team Experience

  • Pain: Technicians arrive on-site without full informationFix: Internal job brief issued 48 hours before every installation — what, where, who, what to bring.
  • Pain: Client calls the CTO directly with support issues after deliveryFix: Support contact is the Account Manager. CTO is escalation only. Communicated in the handover doc.
  • Pain: No visibility on whether a PO or deposit has been receivedFix: Finance confirms payment in the team WhatsApp group before procurement is authorised. One message. Done.
